Continuity-Aligned Rewrite

Knowing that a piece of metal expanded because it was heated is what makes it possible‚Äînot impossible‚Äîto infer the general principle that heating causes expansion.
Laws arise out of singular causal recognitions, not the reverse.

A law, properly understood, expresses a proportional dependence between magnitudes, not an exception-free regularity.
The greater the temperature increase, the greater the expansion; the greater the applied momentum, the greater the displacement.
Regularity is surface; proportionality is structure.

Causal knowledge begins with singular proportional events (the lone dalliance causing the disease, the one unfit run causing the heart attack).
Scientific explanation refines these pre-scientific recognitions by quantifying their ratios, not by replacing them.
Experimentation isolates dependence-relations, clarifying proportional laws without altering their causal foundation.

Since effects follow causes over finite intervals, no sequence constitutes a perfect regularity.
Laws therefore dispose systems toward regular outcomes but never guarantee them.
To know why is to know how much: to understand the proportional structure connecting cause and effect.
Thus, explanation is not subsumption under law but recognition of proportion within singular events.
Regularities are the shadow of dependencies, not their source.
